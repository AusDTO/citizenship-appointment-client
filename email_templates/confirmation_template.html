Prepare for your appointment

  • Collect all originals of the required documents
  • Translate non-english documents

When you arrive

  • Please allow enough travel time to arrive at least 5 minutes prior to your appointment
  • The appointment will take place at #UnitAddress . If you need special assistance, please let us know
  • Collect a ticket at the Citizenship kiosk by entering your Client ID or scanning the barcode on top of this page
  • Wait until your number is called at the assigned counter

You must bring the following original documents

Evidence of when you first arrived in Australia and your present country of citizenship

Current and/or previous passports and travel documents with visas (if applicable).
E.g. Document for Travel to Australia (DFTTA), PLO56 (M56), Titre de Voyage

Evidence of your identity
Birth certificate, and
A document with your photo and sugnature, and
E.g. Australian driver licence, National identity card, current passport, an air crew identity document, seafarer identity document, military identity document, Student Card, Australian Proof of Age card, Australian Keypass card, Working with Children Check card.
A proof of your current residential address
E.g. Utilities notice (such as electricity, gas or water bill), bank notice, rental contract or rates notice.

If you have changed your name or been known by any other names
If you have changed your name or been known by any other names since bith, you must provide official evidence of links between present and previous names.
E.g. Marriage or divorce certificate, Name Change Certificate from the Australian Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages (RBDM) or an official overseas authority.

Good character documents
Overseas penal clearance
You will need to provide original penal clearance certificates from overseas countries if, since the grant of your first permanentn Australian visa:
  • You have lived or travelled overseas since the age of 18 or over, and
  • The total time spent overseas added up to 12 months or more, and
  • The time you spent in any one country was more than 90 days, or
  • You are requested to do so by the department.
If you are applying outside Australia, then Australia should be considered an overseas country. www.border.gov.au/Trav/Visa/Char
In some cases further documents might be requested.

Documents for children

The following documents are required for all children under 16 years of age

A full birth certificate of family register containing parent's details
A Passport or travel document used to enter Australia, if not on your passport
Evidence of any name change between birth and and current name, if applicable
If you have been granted custody and/or guardianship of the child, bring the relevant court orders.
If the child is adopted, bring the decision papers
If you have included children in your application they are not required to attend this appointment. There are no childcare facilities so you will need to make alternative childcare arrangements for your children.

If you have applied online
Form 1195 - Identity declaration
Provide Form 1195 for you and all children included in your application.

Translating your documents
If required documents are not in English, you will also need to provide a translation with the original document.
If you are in Australia when applying, use translators accredited by the National Accreditation Authority of Translatiors and Interpreters (NAATI). For more information see www.naati.com.au
If you are outside of Australia when applying, the English translation must be completed by a qualified translator and must be endorsed by the translator with their full name, address, telephone number and details of their qualifications and experience in the language being translated.

Interview for 60 years and over
During the interview we will ensure that you understand the nature of your citizenship application, unless you are applying on the grounds that you have a physical or mental disability. If you need an interpreter, you can bring a family member or friend to assist you during the interview. You will not need to sit the test.

Interview for 16 and 17 year olds
During the interview, we will ensure that you understand the nature of your citizenship application, unless you are applying on the grounds that you have a physical or mental incapacity.
If you are 16 or 17 years of age we will asses your English language ability and your knowledge of the responsibilities and priviledges of Australian citizenship. You do not need to sit the citizenship test. Please refer to the secion 'How to prepare for the test' which provides the information you need for your assesment.

How to prepare for the test
The citizenship test is computer-based and conducted in English and cosists of 20 mutiple choice questions.
Australian Citizenship: Our Common Bond is your Study Guide, which is available in 37 languages and has all the information you need to help you pass the citizenship test.

Changes to your circumstances
It is important that you tell us about any changes to your circumstances including your name, contact details, address or children included in this application as soon as possible. If you applied online, you can update your details through immi-account. Alternatively, use Form 929 Change of address and/or passport details or contact the Citizenship information line.
