Important notes about your citizenship appointment

  • It is very important that you attend this appointment as this is your opportunity to demonstrate that you meet the legal requirements for citizenship.
  • Please select the soonest available appointment that suits you.
  • You are required to schedule an appointment within 2 weeks of receiving this information.
  • Appointments run for approximately 2 hours.

What will happen at your appointment

  • We will check the original documents for all applicants within your application (including children) and any extra documentation that you have been requested to provide. We will take your photograph and collect your signature.
  • Once we have verified your identity, you will be able to sit the citizenship test.
  • If you have included children in your application they are not required to attend this appointment. There are no childcare facilities so you will need to make alternative childcare arrangements for your children.

You must bring the following original documents
  • The originals of all the identity documents that you submitted with your application, for all applicants including children; and
  • Any other documents that you have been requested to provide.
  • If you lodged your application online, please bring your completed Form 1195 Identity declaration, with an endorsed recent passport sized photo for all applicants including children.
  • If you do not bring the required original documents or provide the completed Form 1195 and an endorsed photo (if lodged online), we will not be able to verify your identity and proceed with your application.

Citizenship test
The citizenship test is computer-based and conducted in English and consists of 20 multiple choice questions. If you do not complete the test within the time allocated, a result will be given based on the questions completed.
 
You must not refer to study aids or electronic equipment such as cameras or smartphones during the test. You may speak to the test administrator but you cannot speak to other people during the test. If you do not follow these rules your test result will be invalid.
 
If you have an assisted test, the test administrator will read out all the questions and the possible answers as many times as you request during the time you have to complete the test. The test administrator will operate the computer for you.
 
If you fail the test you can ask to take the test again. Where possible, you may be allowed to sit the test again on the same day if you have a score of more than 50%. Otherwise, you may make an appointment to sit the test at another time. If you have difficulty passing the test, we may discuss other options with you.
 
Prepare for the test by reading Australian Citizenship: Our Common Bond. It is available in 37 languages and has all the information you need to help you pass the citizenship test.

What happens to your application after your appointment?
After you have attended your appointment, assessment of your application will continue. You may be asked to provide additional information such as other overseas penal clearances and/or you may be asked to attend another interview. The department will advise you in writing of the outcome of your application.

Personal identifiers
Your photograph and signature are called personal identifiers. I am authorised under section 40 of the Australian Citizenship Act 2007 to request personal identifiers from you. Please consider this a formal request for your personal identifiers.
 
Your personal identifiers will be stored electronically in the department's computer system and may be shared with the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade under an agreement we have with them to assist in administering the Australian Passports Act 2005. Your personal identifiers may also be produced in evidence in a court or tribunal in proceedings concerning you.
 
Please note that the Privacy Act 1988 applies to a personal identifier. You have the right to make a complaint to the Australian Information Commissioner about the handling of personal information. Please also note that the Freedom of Information Act 1982 gives you access to certain information and documents held by the Australian Government and its agencies. You have a right under that Act to seek access to that information or documents, and to seek amendment of records containing personal information that is incomplete, incorrect, out of date or misleading.

Changes to your circumstances
It is important that you tell us about any changes to your circumstances including changing your name, contact details, address or information about children included in your application. If you applied online, you can update your details through Immi-account. Alternatively, use Form 929 Change of address and/or passport details or contact the Citizenship Information Line.
